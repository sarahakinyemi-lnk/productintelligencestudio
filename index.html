About

I'm a product researcher and strategist with 16 years in software and a master's in sociology — a pattern hunter who specializes in finding the gap between what you think you know and what's actually true.

That gap is expensive. It lives in your roadmap assumptions, your prioritization debates, and the product bets you're about to make. When you close it, you don't just get better research — you get a team that builds with confidence, makes faster decisions, and stops second-guessing the direction.

I've worked on AI products, enterprise software, B2B SaaS, and startup mobile applications. I know how to walk into an organization, build trust and alignment, and surface the insights that change how teams build.

Case Study

Late 2025 — AI Startup

An AI startup was seeing low adoption despite strong initial excitement. Users said they loved the product, but weren't using it at the weekly or even monthly intervals the team had hoped to see. They assumed it was a UX problem and set to work fine-tuning the experience.

User research told a different story: the tool was so specialized that it was only relevant to users a few times a year. It wasn't broken — it was mispositioned.

That single insight completely reframed how the team measured success, what they prioritized, and what they decided to build next.

FAQ

What does a sprint actually cost?

Pricing depends on the scope of the engagement. Most sprints fall in the range of $5-10K. Book an intro call and I'll give you a straight answer based on what you need.

How long does an engagement take?

Most research sprints run 2–3 weeks from kickoff to final readout. Some scopes are shorter. I'll give you a timeline estimate based on your questions and constraints.

Do you do the interviews yourself, or do we?

Either. I can conduct interviews directly, or I can review recordings and transcripts your team has already collected. Often it's a mix of both.

We already do user research internally. How is this different?

Bravo! Internal research is valuable, and I will gladly pull from it. If I find your research to be unbiased, I may shake your hand and advise you to keep doing what you're doing. If I discover your research is too shallow or has blind spots (ofen fueled by confirmation bias, limited bandwidth, and proximity to the product) then I'll recommend a sprint. I come in as a neutral outsider who knows what questions to ask and how to pressure-test the answers.

We're pre-seed. Are we too early?

Probably, but it depends. Reach out and we'll figure it out. The intro call is free and I'll tell you honestly if the timing isn't right.

Who are some product leaders you're influenced by?

Marty Cagan and the Silicon Valley Product Group, and Teresa Torres of Product Talk — if you've read Inspired or Continuous Discovery Habits, you'll notice their thought leadership in my work. They both push hard on the idea that great product teams stay in continuous contact with their users. That resonates deeply with me.
